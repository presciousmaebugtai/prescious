"Pokemon" is a media franchise created by Nintendo, Game Freak, and Creatures. It was first introduced by Nintendo in 1996 and has since become one of the most successful and recognizable franchises in the world. The name "Pokemon" is a romanized contraction of the Japanese brand "Pocket Monsters", often shortened as "PokeMon" in Japanese.

The franchise revolves around fictional creatures called "Pokemon," which humans, known as Pokemon Trainers, catch and train to battle each other for sport. The main goal for Pokemon Trainers is often to become a Pokemon Master by capturing and training a diverse team of Pokemon, earning Gym Badges, and ultimately challenging the Elite Four and the Pokemon League.
